About Us

At Mercedes-Benz our employees and communities are at the heart of everything we do


Mercedes-Benz, often simply referred to as Mercedes or Benz, is a German luxury automotive brand with a history that is synonymous with the invention and evolution of the automobile itself. Headquartered in Stuttgart, Baden-Württemberg, Germany, the company operates as a subsidiary of the Mercedes-Benz Group AG. Known for its slogan "The Best or Nothing," it is recognized globally as a premium manufacturer of luxury cars, vans, and, historically, heavy commercial vehicles.

Historical Origins and Legacy (1886–1926)

The First Automobile:

The roots of Mercedes-Benz trace back to 1886, when Karl Benz patented the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, widely regarded as the first internal combustion-powered automobile. In the same year, Gottlieb Daimler developed his own four-wheeled motorized carriage, effectively establishing the two pillars of the company.

The Name "Mercedes":

The name "Mercedes" originates from 1900, derived from Mercédès Jellinek, the daughter of Austrian businessman Emil Jellinek, who ordered a special, high-performance car from Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft (DMG). This car, the Mercedes 35 PS, was so successful that "Mercedes" became a brand name for DMG.

The Merger:

Due to economic instability in Germany after World War I, Benz & Cie. and DMG merged in 1926 to form Daimler-Benz AG, launching the combined brand name "Mercedes-Benz".

The Three-Pointed Star Symbol

The famous three-pointed star, designed by Gottlieb Daimler, symbolizes the brand’s ambition for motorization in three areas: on land, on water, and in the air. The star-in-a-circle logo was formally registered in 1937 and has since become one of the most recognized symbols of quality and prestige in the world.

Engineering Prowess and Safety Leadership

Safety Innovations:

The company introduced the safety body with rigid passenger cell and crumple zones in 1959. It was the first manufacturer to introduce the anti-lock braking system (ABS) in 1978 and airbags/seatbelt pretensioners in 1981.

Key "Firsts":

Mercedes-Benz produced the world's first series-production diesel passenger car (260 D) in 1936.

Performance (AMG):

The AMG division, which started as an independent tuning firm, was officially integrated into the company in 1999 (later becoming Mercedes-AMG), delivering high-performance, sport-oriented luxury vehicles.

Modern Brand Structure and Portfolio

As of 2022, the company underwent a restructuring, separating its commercial vehicle business (Daimler Truck) from its passenger car and van business, which is now managed by Mercedes-Benz AG. The portfolio currently includes:

Core Mercedes-Benz Cars:

A, B, C, E, and S-Class sedans, coupes, and estates.

SUVs:

GLA, GLB, GLC, GLE, GLS, and the iconic G-Class.

Electric (EQ):

The EQ series (EQA, EQB, EQE, EQS) represents the brand's shift toward electric mobility, with the Vision EQXX recently setting records for range.

Mercedes-Maybach:

The ultra-luxury sub-brand for high-end S-Class and GLS models.
